THE FUTURE IS MOTION

So what is all the fuss about? And why does it matter?
Motion tells stories. Everything on a page is a sequence, and motion is your guide. For every button clicked and screen transition, there is a story that follows.

Where It Came From

Traditional animation techniques on the web required use of specialized animating software like Adobe Flash or Microsoft Silverlight. Much has changed in the development of motion graphics for the web over the past couple of years. It’s hard to say who started, or what started interaction design. Rather, there are many thought leaders that dove into the world of user-centric design and great web design. There are many new JavaScript tools and libraries available today that are making the creation of motion graphics much easier and less time consuming than previous technologies.

JQuery is a good example of the new JavaScript components that provide practical applications like transitions between images, sliding text, and object effects. Even modal dialog boxes are much easier to implement and give a rich, user experience without a lot of development time.

Where We Can Learn More

It is awesome to see more and more people playing with Material Design. The Google visual language is much more than visual design, it is comprehensive and it blurs really well the lines that separate visual, UX and motion. It also an incentive for new designers to sharpen their skills and focus more and more on becoming great product designers. More information about Material Design can be found on forums and blogs, such as MaterialUp, a great site that aggregates explorations on Google’s design language.

The Future of Motion: Material Design

It’s just a matter of time that we will witness more and more UX and App Designers adapting and adjusting their apps to fit Google’s Material Design on Android and iOS platforms. If Google continues their effort to push Material Design into their products, then its popularity will only keep growing.
Material Design was written with the web, smartphones, tablets and wearables in mind. But Google will definitely go further than that. The Material Design Guidelines already has a section updated with specs that can be applied to television, automobile dashboards and even Google Cardboard, allowing designers to create Material VR apps.

Reviews

Motion design thinking can be applied beyond the screen to different media from services to products. The message is about balancing function and aesthetic. By utilizing this as a building block for design, designers will begin thinking about how to improve future product-user communication across the board.

A key part of designing good UI animations is remembering that adding animation is an opportunity to communicate a little something more. Any motion you add is going to communicate something; it’s really more a question of whether it’s saying what you’d like it to say.

Insight

Things are different now. CSS3 transitions and animations can do most of the heavy lifting and HTML5 audio can serve up the music and audio clips. You can do a lot without leaning on JavaScript at all, and when you lean on JavaScript, you can do so much more!

If an animation doesn’t help the user in some way, by showing them where they are or how two elements on a page relate to each other, then it’s using up battery juice and processing cycles solely for the purpose of delight. Hardly the best use of resources.

Creating a motion language for your application will reinforce your brand and give it personality. Is it bouncy and fun? Secure and heavy? Smooth and professional? The animation will compliment your design and brand identity.
